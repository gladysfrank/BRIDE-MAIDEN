## The World
<details> <summary>Click to expand world details (contains minor spoilers)</summary> <br>
Welcome to Auralith, a planet built on a fragile lie. 
 
Here, truth is whatever the records say… and the records are always changing.

Controlled by the Grand Revision Authority, Auralith lives by one unbreakable law: History is always correct. The past is not
remembered, it is rewritten to keep order.

At the center of this system is the Living Record Division.
Cherry Double-Cross, Warden of the Records, is tasked with keeping the official version of reality intact. Her job: scrub what is
inconvenient, rewrite the past, and erase anything that threatens the lie.

Protecting this fragile order are the Battle Bridemaidens, elite warriors, living symbols of unity, both revered and feared.
Beyond the city walls, outside the Authority’s reach, a small group called the Order of the Silver Rod still quietly preserves life and
principle, refusing to serve the lie.

But no matter how tightly the Authority controls the records, unrest is growing. Petitions disappear. Complaints are silenced.

</details>

## Unique Selling Points

 - **A Story About Rewriting Reality:** A narrative-driven visual novel where you
play as a record keeper who edits history itself, shaping what is allowed to
exist
 - **Morally Complex, Choice-Driven Narrative:** Your decisions shape multiple routes and endings in a world with no clear heroes or villains, only consequences.
 - **Old-School Denpa Storytelling:** A psychological narrative rooted in themes of isolation, delusion, and the erosion of reality, where ordinary routines gradually give way to distorted perception and inner collapse
 - **Stylized Retro Presentation:** An atmospheric experience told in retro pixel art,
animations, and nostalgic chiptune music inspired by PC-98’s OPNA soundchip

## Target Audience
- Fans of psychological denpa visual novels who enjoy stories centered on the slow erosion of truth, fracturing reality, delusion, and psychological unease.
- Players who appreciate morally complex narratives, flawed protagonists, and themes of loyalty, betrayal, propaganda, censorship, and survival in oppressive systems.
- Lovers of ornate fantasy-sci-fi worlds with feudal-inspired power structures and beautiful yet decadent aesthetics (in the style of Yoshitaka Amano, CLAMP, The Five Star Stories, or similar).
= Visual novel enthusiasts who value old-school immersive storytelling, meaningful branching choices, and multiple endings.
- Mature players (18+) looking for atmospheric, story-driven experiences with dark psychological undertones rather than romance or high-action gameplay.
